# SYNTHETIC CASE — generated for RAG pipeline testing

A 31-year-old woman presents with crampy lower abdominal pain that she has had,
on and off, for more than two years. The pain tends to ease after she opens her
bowels and is accompanied by bloating and a sense of incomplete emptying. Her
bowel habit swings between loose stools and constipation, often within the same
week. Symptoms flare during stressful periods at work and around her menstrual
cycle. There is no blood in her stool, no weight loss, and no waking at night
with pain.

She is otherwise well, works in a high-pressure law firm, and takes only
occasional paracetamol and a probiotic she read about online. She has had blood
tests and even a normal colonoscopy arranged by a previous doctor, all of which
were reassuring.

She is anxious and frustrated, and arrives almost defensive. She feels previous
doctors dismissed her as "just stressed" without taking the disruption to her
life seriously, and she is worried something has been missed. The
unpredictability of her symptoms is affecting her confidence at work and her
social life.

On examination her abdomen is soft with mild generalized tenderness and no
masses or alarm features. The underlying cause is irritable bowel syndrome. The
chronic, relapsing pain relieved by defecation, altered bowel habit, stress
correlation, and consistently normal investigations fit a functional disorder.
