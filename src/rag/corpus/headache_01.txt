# SYNTHETIC CASE — generated for RAG pipeline testing

A 27-year-old woman presents with a severe headache that has been building for
the past six hours. The pain is throbbing, concentrated over her right temple,
and rated 8 out of 10. Bright light and noise are unbearable, and she feels
nauseated. About twenty minutes before the pain began she noticed shimmering
zig-zag lines drifting across her vision, which faded as the headache set in.
She has retreated to a dark, quiet room.

She has had similar headaches since her late teens, perhaps once a month, often
around her period or after a poor night's sleep. Her mother and sister get the
same kind. She takes simple painkillers, which help only if she catches an
attack early, and she avoids known triggers like red wine and skipped meals.

She is distressed and tearful, mostly from the pain and from frustration: she
has an important exam tomorrow and knows this will wipe out the rest of her day.
She has already had to leave work early twice this month for the same reason.

On examination she is neurologically intact between the visual symptoms and the
headache, with no focal deficit. The underlying cause is migraine with aura.
The unilateral throbbing pain, photophobia and phonophobia, nausea, preceding
visual aura, hormonal and sleep triggers, and strong family history are
characteristic.
