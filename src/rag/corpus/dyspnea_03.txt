# SYNTHETIC CASE — generated for RAG pipeline testing

A 45-year-old woman presents with four days of worsening breathlessness, fever,
and a cough productive of yellow-green phlegm. She points to a sharp pain low on
the right side of her chest that stabs when she breathes deeply or coughs. She
feels hot, drained, and has had shaking chills overnight. A mild head cold the
week before seemed to settle and then suddenly turned worse.

She is generally healthy, works as a primary school teacher, and takes only an
occasional antihistamine. She does not smoke. She mentions, almost in passing,
that everything feels harder because she is a single parent of two young
children and has no one to help while she is unwell.

She is anxious and exhausted. Her main worry is not herself but who will collect
the children from school and whether she can afford to take time off work. She
keeps trying to minimize how ill she feels.

On examination she is febrile at 38.9°C, breathing faster than normal, with
bronchial breath sounds and crackles over the right lower lung field. The
underlying cause is community-acquired pneumonia. The acute fever, productive
cough, pleuritic chest pain, and focal chest findings following a viral
prodrome point to a bacterial lower respiratory tract infection.
