# SYNTHETIC CASE — generated for RAG pipeline testing

A 60-year-old man presents with a headache that has been present since he woke
this morning, felt mainly at the back of his head. He also reports feeling
generally unwell, slightly dizzy, and says his vision has seemed a little blurry
at times. He cannot recall having headaches like this before. A practice nurse
checked his blood pressure at a pharmacy yesterday and told him it was "very
high" and that he should see someone.

He was diagnosed with hypertension several years ago and started on tablets, but
he stopped taking them many months ago because he "felt fine" and disliked the
side effects. He has not seen his doctor in over a year. He works as a
long-distance lorry driver, eats mostly at roadside cafés, and adds salt to most
meals.

He is dismissive and a little impatient, insisting he has "always been
healthy" and that the fuss is unnecessary. He is reluctant to accept that
anything is wrong and is worried mainly about losing his driving medical.

On examination his blood pressure is markedly elevated at 212/124 mmHg, with an
otherwise non-focal neurological examination. The underlying cause is a
hypertensive headache from severely uncontrolled blood pressure, driven by
stopped medication, and requires careful evaluation and treatment.
