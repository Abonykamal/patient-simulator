# SYNTHETIC CASE — generated for RAG pipeline testing

A 22-year-old university student presents with episodes of breathlessness and
wheezing that have worsened over the past month. She describes a tight chest
and an audible whistle when she breathes out, along with a dry cough that wakes
her in the early hours of the morning. Symptoms flare during exercise, in cold
air, and when she visits a friend who owns two cats. Between episodes she feels
completely well.

She has a childhood history of eczema and hay fever, and her mother has asthma.
She owns a blue reliever inhaler but admits she lost it weeks ago and has been
managing without. She does not smoke but lives with two flatmates who do, and
their flat is often smoky.

She is frustrated and a little frightened. The night-time cough is exhausting
her before exams, and last week she had to stop halfway through a run, gasping,
which scared her badly. She is anxious about having a serious attack when she is
alone.

On examination she has a scattered expiratory wheeze and her peak flow is
reduced, improving after a bronchodilator. The underlying cause is asthma. The
reversible airflow limitation, nocturnal and exercise-triggered symptoms, atopic
background, and family history together make the diagnosis clear and highlight
the need to restart inhaled therapy.
